# Global Escalation State Snapshot

**Snapshot date:** 2026-05-02
**Purpose:** Feeds Prophet's threat-timing forecaster. Adversaries time cyber operations against military and diplomatic tempo; this is the live "open conflicts and tensions" picture, with cyber-relevant detail on each actor.
**Coverage window:** Where "recent" appears, this means the last 90 days unless otherwise noted. Cyber section covers Nov 2025 – May 2026. Diplomatic section covers Nov 2025 – May 2026.

## 1. Active Armed Conflicts

### Russia–Ukraine (state-on-state, full-scale war, year 5)
**Parties:** Russian Federation vs. Ukraine. North Korean troop rotations, Iranian Shahed drone supply, and Belarusian territorial logistics support remain on the Russian side; NATO members provide weapons, intel, and training to Ukraine.
**Current state:** Russia's spring–summer 2026 offensive is largely blunted. Per ISW/Critical Threats (April 30, 2026), Russia suffered a *net loss* of ~26 sq mi over March 31–April 28; Russian gains slowed to roughly 5 km/day in Q1 2026 vs. 11 km/day in Q1 2025, with Ukrainian counter-advances around Kostyantynivka–Druzhkivka geolocated April 30. Russia's "spring offensive" is grinding, not breakthroughs. ([ISW – Critical Threats, April 30, 2026](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/russian-offensive-campaign-assessment-april-30-2026); [Kyiv Post / ISW April 30](https://www.kyivpost.com/post/75144))
**Recent escalation events (last 90 days):**
- **April 29, 2026** – UK expels a Russian diplomat in retaliation for Moscow expelling a British official; tit-for-tat reflecting "spiraling tensions." ([US News / AP](https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2026-04-29/uk-expels-russian-diplomat-in-retaliation-for-moscows-recent-expulsion-of-a-british-official))
- **May 2, 2026** – Two Romanian F-16s scrambled after ~20 Russian drones approached NATO airspace, "some objects" crossed into Romanian territory. ([Wikipedia – Violations of non-combatant airspace, ongoing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Violations_of_non-combatant_airspace_during_the_Russo-Ukrainian_war_(2022%E2%80%93present)))
- **April 4, 2026** – Russia again claimed Luhansk Oblast as fully captured; CNN/ISW assess this is propaganda, real control is partial. ([CNN, April 4 2026](https://www.cnn.com/2026/04/04/europe/russia-ukraine-luhansk-intl))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** GRU Unit 74455 (Sandworm / APT44) remains the most disruptive actor in this conflict. ESET attributed the Dec 29–30, 2025 wiper attack on Polish CHP plants and a renewables management system to Sandworm with medium confidence — the new wiper is named **DynoWiper** and shows TTP overlap with the prior ZOV wiper used in Ukraine. ([ESET WeLiveSecurity](https://www.welivesecurity.com/en/eset-research/eset-research-sandworm-cyberattack-poland-power-grid-late-2025/); [Dark Reading](https://www.darkreading.com/threat-intelligence/sandworm-wiper-attack-poland-power-grid)) Sandworm's playbook continues to be CADDYWIPER-class destruction in Ukraine plus disruptive ops against NATO-aligned energy and logistics. ([Mandiant / Google Cloud](https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/gru-disruptive-playbook))

### Israel–Gaza / Lebanon / Iran complex
**Parties:** Israel, Hamas, Hezbollah, Iran (state), and Houthis (Yemen) plus US (active strike role since Feb 2026).
**Current state:** Three overlapping ceasefires, all fragile.
- The **Twelve-Day War / 2026 Iran war** began Feb 28, 2026 with US/Israeli airstrikes (codenamed Operation Roaring Lion / Operation Epic Fury). Strikes killed Iran's supreme leader and other senior officials and damaged nuclear/ballistic facilities. A US–Israel–Iran ceasefire was announced April 7 (effective April 8 local), then extended ~3 weeks. Both sides accuse the other of breaches; small-scale exchanges continue. Trump on April 13 ordered a naval blockade after the Islamabad talks collapsed; Iran has refused to fully reopen the Strait of Hormuz. ([Britannica overview](https://www.britannica.com/event/2026-Iran-war); [Wikipedia – 2026 Iran war ceasefire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Iran_war_ceasefire); [CBS News live updates](https://www.cbsnews.com/live-updates/iran-war-trump-strait-of-hormuz-israel-lebanon-ceasefire/))
- The **Israel–Lebanon ceasefire** began April 16, 2026 (10-day cessation, brokered by US), extended to ~3 weeks on April 23. By mid-April, >2,000 Lebanese killed, >1M displaced. IDF chief stated late April there is "no ceasefire" in south Lebanon — strikes continue. Hezbollah is not a signatory. ([Wikipedia – 2026 Israel–Lebanon ceasefire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Israel%E2%80%93Lebanon_ceasefire); [UN News](https://news.un.org/en/story/2026/04/1167375); [Times of Israel](https://www.timesofisrael.com/idf-chief-says-theres-no-ceasefire-in-south-lebanon-amid-continued-fighting-with-hezbollah/))
- **Gaza** remains under the Oct 10, 2025 ceasefire framework, but deadlock over Hamas weapons and post-war governance threatens it. ([Al Jazeera, April 26 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/26/what-lies-ahead-for-gaza-after-ceasefires-in-iran-and-lebanon))

**Houthi (Yemen) sub-front:** Houthis joined the war in early April with missile/drone strikes on Israel, then paused. They have *not* resumed sustained Red Sea shipping attacks despite earlier February 2026 statements they would. They have explicitly threatened to close the Bab-el-Mandeb if Gulf states join the war. Bab-el-Mandeb still handles ~12% of global seaborne oil. ([Military Times, April 14 2026](https://www.militarytimes.com/news/pentagon-congress/2026/04/14/amid-focus-on-strait-of-hormuz-experts-sound-warning-on-yemens-houthis-and-red-sea/); [Fox News](https://www.foxnews.com/politics/second-choke-point-crisis-looms-houthis-threaten-key-red-sea-shipping-lane); [MARAD MSCI 2026-006](https://www.maritime.dot.gov/msci/2026-006-red-sea-bab-el-mandeb-strait-gulf-aden-arabian-sea-and-somali-basin-houthi-attacks))

**Cyber-relevant aspects:** The Iran war triggered the largest spike in Iran-attributed offensive cyber activity since 2020. Key items:
- **Stryker Corporation** wiper-style destructive op on **March 11, 2026** — destructive operation against a US target executed *without malware* by abusing legitimate MDM infrastructure. Industry analysts assess this as a qualitative escalation in Iranian operational tradecraft. ([SOCRadar dashboard](https://socradar.io/iran-israel-cyber-conflict-dashboard/))
- **Handala Hack** (linked to MOIS) compromised Israeli energy firms, Jordanian fuel systems, and healthcare targets. ([SOCRadar](https://socradar.io/blog/cyber-reflections-us-israel-iran-war/))
- Canadian Centre for Cyber Security issued a Feb 2026 bulletin specifically warning of Iranian cyber threat response to US/Israel strikes. ([Canadian Centre for Cyber Security](https://www.cyber.gc.ca/en/guidance/cyber-threat-bulletin-iranian-cyber-threat-response-usisrael-strikes-february-2026))
- Palo Alto Unit 42 maintains an active "Threat Brief: Escalation of Cyber Risk Related to Iran" (last updated April 17). ([Unit 42](https://unit42.paloaltonetworks.com/iranian-cyberattacks-2026/))

### Sudan civil war (SAF vs. RSF)
**Parties:** Sudanese Armed Forces (SAF) vs. Rapid Support Forces (RSF). State backers: UAE materially supports RSF (publicly alleged); Egypt, Saudi Arabia tilt SAF; Iran has provided drones to SAF.
**Current state:** Three years in. SAF-led government returned to Khartoum on **Jan 11, 2026**. RSF holds El Fasher (captured Oct 26, 2025); UN/HRW report mass atrocities, ethnically motivated killings, and torture in RSF detention centers there. ~8.6M IDPs and ~3M refugees — the world's largest displacement crisis. Famine confirmed in El Fasher and Kadugli. Fighting now intense in Kordofan with near-daily drone strikes. ([CFR Global Conflict Tracker](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/power-struggle-sudan); [Al Jazeera, April 27 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/27/thousands-reportedly-held-by-paramilitary-rsf-in-sudans-el-fasher); [Soufan Center, April 21 2026](https://thesoufancenter.org/intelbrief-2026-april-21/); [HRW World Report 2026](https://www.hrw.org/world-report/2026/country-chapters/sudan))
**Recent escalation events:** Berlin aid conference April 2026 — 61 countries pledged €1.5B and called for ceasefire (no movement). Drone strikes on markets and health facilities throughout Q1 2026.
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** Limited public attribution. UAE is the most plausible cyber-enabled supporter on the RSF side; SAF–Iran link raises possibility of Iranian-supplied capability for disinformation. No major firm has publicly attributed a wiper campaign to this conflict. Lower cyber tempo than the other active wars — relevant as a baseline contrast.

### Myanmar civil war (year 5)
**Parties:** State Administration Council (SAC / military junta / Tatmadaw) vs. People's Defence Force (PDF), National Unity Government (NUG), and ethnic armed organizations (KIA, AA, TNLA, MNDAA, KNLA, others).
**Current state:** Junta controls <40% of townships; resistance/EAOs control ~42%. Junta-aligned party "won" sham January 2026 elections; new military-backed civilian-fronted government took over April 2026. SAC announced an April 2–22 ceasefire that it then violated with airstrikes. Resistance has slowed under Chinese pressure on northern EAOs and reduced weapons flows. ACLED tallied >15,000 conflict deaths in 2025; ~5.2M displaced. ([Washington Post, April 29 2026](https://www.washingtonpost.com/world/2026/04/29/myanmar-civil-war-tatmadaw/857a98c2-4445-11f1-b19d-32431046b5b4_story.html); [FDD, April 15 2026](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2026/04/15/new-resistance-alliance-built-to-win-myanmars-civil-war/); [Al Jazeera, March 27 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/features/2026/3/27/who-is-fighting-in-myanmars-multi-front-civil-war))
**Named state actors:** Junta is dependent on China, Russia, and Iran for survival.
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** Largely an internal conflict from a cyber-attribution standpoint — Chinese cyber influence operations support junta narratives but are not publicly attributed at incident granularity to this conflict. Lower priority for the threat-timing model.

### Ethiopia – Tigray (renewed risk)
**Parties:** Ethiopian National Defense Force (ENDF) vs. Tigray People's Liberation Front (TPLF); Eritrea independent variable.
**Current state:** Not yet open war but at the edge. Late January 2026 saw clashes; ENDF amassed heavy artillery and tanks at the Tigray border in early February. TPLF restored Tigray's regional government in April 2026 — a major political escalation. Ethiopia formally acknowledged Eritrean troops' role in the prior war (Feb 2026) while also accusing Eritrea of currently occupying Sheraro and Gulomakada. ([Critical Threats Africa File, April 9 2026](https://www.criticalthreats.org/analysis/ethiopia-tplf-tia-ethiopia-gerd-egypt-somalia-jubbaland-southwest-madagascar-russia-drc-mdkc-m23-jnim-issp-nigeria-sahel-africa-file-april-9-2026); [Al Jazeera, April 20 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/20/fears-over-ethiopia-peace-deal-as-tplf-restores-tigray-government); [JURIST, Feb 2026](https://www.jurist.org/news/2026/02/ethiopia-acknowledges-eritrean-troops-role-in-tigray-war-as-regional-tensions-rise/))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** No major attributed cyber ops, but worth flagging given that the prior Tigray war saw extensive Ethiopian/Eritrean information operations.

## 2. Hot Flashpoints Without Active War

### Taiwan Strait / PRC–Taiwan
**Parties:** PRC (PLA Eastern Theater Command), ROC (Taiwan), with US/Japan as backstop.
**Current posture (April 2026):** **169 PLA aerial incursions into Taiwan ADIZ in April alone.** On **April 20**, the PLA Navy sailed the carrier *Liaoning* through the Taiwan Strait into the SCS while simultaneously dispatching a naval task group through the Yokoate Channel into the Western Pacific — both timed against Balikatan 2026. On **April 18**, China conducted joint air-naval combat readiness patrols in the East China Sea the day after a Japanese MSDF transit of the Strait. ([AEI China & Taiwan Update, May 1 2026](https://www.aei.org/articles/china-taiwan-update-may-1-2026/); [AEI April 17 2026](https://www.aei.org/articles/china-taiwan-update-april-17-2026/); [Japan Times, April 18 2026](https://www.japantimes.co.jp/news/2026/04/18/asia-pacific/china-east-china-sea-military-exercises/))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** This is the highest-stakes flashpoint for the threat-timing model. **Volt Typhoon** (PRC, espionage/positioning) is pre-positioned in US comms, energy, transportation, and water IT systems for disruption "at a time of their choosing" — explicitly Taiwan-contingency-relevant per ODNI 2026 Annual Threat Assessment. **Salt Typhoon** (PRC MSS) compromised 9+ US telecoms (Verizon, AT&T, T-Mobile, Charter/Spectrum, Lumen, Consolidated, Windstream) and exfiltrated metadata on ~1M Americans including senior officials; FBI confirmed in Feb 2026 the threat is *active and ongoing*. Norwegian PST disclosed in its Feb 2026 annual threat assessment that Salt Typhoon also compromised Norwegian network devices. ([CISA China advisories](https://www.cisa.gov/topics/cyber-threats-and-advisories/nation-state-cyber-actors/china); [The Record / Recorded Future](https://therecord.media/norawy-intelligence-discloses-salt-typhoon-attacks); [IISS, Jan 2026](https://www.iiss.org/online-analysis/cyber-power-matrix/2026/01/volt-typhoons-long-shadow/))

### Korean peninsula
**Parties:** DPRK vs. ROK + US Forces Korea + Japan.
**Current posture:** Pyongyang fired its **7th ballistic missile of 2026** by April 19 (4th in April alone). April 19 launch: 5 tactical Hwasong-11 variants hit a target ~136 km out with high precision. Earlier April test included missiles with **cluster-bomb warheads**, anti-aircraft systems, claimed electromagnetic weapons, and carbon-fiber bombs. ROK NSC convened emergency meeting; Japan formally protested. ([NPR, April 18](https://www.npr.org/2026/04/18/nx-s1-5789939/north-korea-ballistic-missiles); [The Diplomat](https://thediplomat.com/2026/04/north-korea-fires-5-tactical-ballistic-missiles/); [Stars and Stripes](https://www.stripes.com/theaters/asia_pacific/2026-04-20/north-korea-missiles-cluster-warhead-21431148.html))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** DPRK Lazarus / Bluenoroff / Andariel (RGB) continue financially-motivated cyber ops as primary tempo. **April 18, 2026** – KelpDAO/LayerZero exploit, ~$290M crypto theft, preliminary attribution to Lazarus Group per industry reporting. North American crypto firm intrusion detected by Arctic Wolf Labs starting Jan 23, 2026. Mandiant's framework now treats "Lazarus" as an umbrella term across multiple RGB sub-units. ([UPI, April 22 2026](https://www.upi.com/Top_News/World-News/2026/04/22/KelpDAO-LayerZero-North-Korea-crypto-hack-theft-Lazarus-Group/6151776848419/); [Mandiant – DPRK mapping](https://cloud.google.com/blog/topics/threat-intelligence/mapping-dprk-groups-to-government/))

### India–Pakistan
**Parties:** Republic of India, Islamic Republic of Pakistan; both nuclear-armed.
**Current posture:** Following the April 22, 2025 Pahalgam attack (26 civilians killed) and India's May 6, 2025 **Operation Sindoor** strikes on 9 sites in Pakistan / Pakistan-administered Kashmir (first-ever drone-on-drone exchange between the two), a US-brokered ceasefire ended that round. India remains withdrawn from the Indus Waters Treaty. A surprise handshake in Dhaka (Jan 2026) and CFR's 2026 survey both flag renewed conflict as a top risk. Militant focus has shifted from Kashmir Valley to Jammu division per ACLED. ([CFR Global Conflict Tracker](https://www.cfr.org/global-conflict-tracker/conflict/conflict-between-india-and-pakistan); [ACLED](https://acleddata.com/report/jammu-and-kashmir-new-risks-security-forces-and-civilians-threaten-regional-peace); [Al Jazeera, Jan 2 2026](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/1/2/handshake-in-dhaka-can-india-and-pakistan-revive-ties-in-2026))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** Pakistani APTs (Transparent Tribe / APT36, SideCopy) and Indian-aligned groups (Patchwork, SideWinder) trade espionage ops; tempo correlates tightly with kinetic flare-ups.

### South China Sea (Philippines incidents)
**Parties:** PRC (PLA Navy, Coast Guard, maritime militia) vs. Philippines (with US, Japan, Australia under MDT and reciprocal agreements).
**Current posture:** Major escalation at Scarborough Shoal in April 2026:
- **April 10–11, 2026** – Reuters-imaged satellite shots: PRC deployed a 352 m / 1,150 ft floating barrier across the entrance to Scarborough Shoal, plus 10 CCG vessels Apr 5–12.
- **Apr 20–May 8** – PRC conducted combat-readiness patrols and "dangerous and provocative" intercepts of a Philippine military aircraft over a disputed reef during Balikatan 2026; Marcos publicly condemned.
- **April 30, 2026** – PLA naval/air combat readiness patrols specifically near Scarborough, framed as response to Balikatan. ([USNews / Reuters, April 30](https://www.usnews.com/news/world/articles/2026-04-30/china-conducts-combat-readiness-patrols-in-scarborough-shoal); [Vision Times, April 20](https://www.visiontimes.com/2026/04/20/china-builds-barricade-at-scarborough-shoal-raising-south-china-sea-tensions-with-philippines.html); [FDD, April 16](https://www.fdd.org/analysis/2026/04/15/new-resistance-alliance-built-to-win-myanmars-civil-war/))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** PRC cyber pressure on Philippines correlates with Scarborough/Second Thomas Shoal events; Volt Typhoon-style positioning is the strategic backdrop.

### Sahel (Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger) and Africa Corps
**Parties:** AES juntas (Mali, Burkina Faso, Niger) + Russian Africa Corps vs. JNIM (al-Qaeda) and ISGS, plus Tuareg Azawad Liberation Front (FLA) in Mali.
**Current posture:** **April 25–26, 2026** – Coordinated FLA + JNIM attacks across Kidal, Gao, Sevare, and Kati; **Malian Defense Minister Sadio Camara and family members were killed.** FLA forced Africa Corps units out of Kidal — a strategic setback for Russia. ~2,000 Russian fighters in Mali, ~100 in Niger, 100–300 in Burkina Faso. ([Al Jazeera, April 29](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/4/29/what-role-has-russia-played-in-malis-security-and-the-sahel-region); [The Hill](https://thehill.com/opinion/national-security/5857069-russia-africa-corps-influence/); [RFE/RL](https://www.rferl.org/a/russia-africa-corps-mali-withdrawal-wagner-mercenaries/33744461.html))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** Russian-aligned info-ops (Doppelganger, GRU-adjacent personas) saturate Sahel social media. Africa Corps' growing setbacks may push Moscow toward more aggressive disinformation rather than less.

### Caucasus (Armenia–Azerbaijan)
**Parties:** Armenia, Azerbaijan; Russia (declining), US (newly significant), Turkey, Iran.
**Current posture:** Peace agreement initialed (March 13, 2025) and TRIPP corridor framework brokered by Trump (Aug 2025) — but signature/operational implementation depends on Armenia's 2027 constitutional referendum and PM Pashinyan's parliamentary fortunes in 2026. TRIPP construction on Armenian soil expected to begin 2026; US has 99-year development rights. Russian forces have withdrawn from the Armenia–Azerbaijan border per the agreement. ([CACI Analyst, 2026 entry](https://www.cacianalyst.org/publications/analytical-articles/item/13917-the-armenia-azerbaijan-peace-process-enters-2026.html); [RAND, Jan 2026](https://www.rand.org/pubs/commentary/2026/01/what-prospects-for-lasting-peace-between-armenia-and-azerbaijan.html); [Carnegie, March 2026](https://carnegieendowment.org/research/2026/03/rewiring-the-south-caucasus-tripp-and-the-new-geopolitics-of-connectivity))
**Cyber-relevant aspects:** Currently lower threat tempo than during 2023–2024 Karabakh phase. Russia's reduced position incentivizes hybrid (info-ops, possible critical-infrastructure probing) to retain leverage.
